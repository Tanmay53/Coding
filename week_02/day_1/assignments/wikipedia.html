Linus Benedict Torvalds (/'li:nəs'tɔːrvɔːldz/ LEE-nəs TOR-vawldz,[5] Finland Swedish: [ˈliːnʉs ˈtuːrvɑlds] (About this soundlisten); born December 28, 1969) is a Finnish-American software engineer who is the creator and, historically, the principal developer of the Linux kernel, which is the kernel for Linux operating systems (distributions) and other operating systems such as Android and Chrome OS. He also created the distributed version control system Git and the scuba dive loggig and planning software Subsurface.
He was hanoured, along with Shinya Yamanaka, with the 2012 Millenium Technology Prize by the Technology Academy Finland "in recognition of a new open source operating system for computers leading to the widely used Linux kernel." He is also the recipient of the 2014 IEEE Computer Society Computer Pioneer Award and the 2018 IEEE Masaru Ibuka Consumer Electronics Award.
